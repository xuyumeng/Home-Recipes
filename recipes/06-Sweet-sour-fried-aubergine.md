### Ingredients
- Eggplant
- Garlic (minced)
- Green onion (chopped)
- Dried chili peppers
- Sichuan peppercorns (optional)
- Cilantro (cut into sections)
- Starch (preferred for coating)

### Sauce
- 3 tbsp sugar
- 3 tbsp white vinegar
- 3 tbsp light soy sauce
- 3 tbsp water
- 1 tbsp oyster sauce (optional)
- A pinch of salt  
(Mix well and adjust to taste)

---

## Instructions

### 1. Prepare the Eggplant
- Wash the eggplant and cut it into evenly sized wedges.
- Remove the inner pulp if desired, then cut into diamond-shaped pieces.
- Soak in cold water for about 10 minutes to absorb moisture and keep a tender texture.
- Meanwhile, prepare garlic, green onion, dried chili, Sichuan peppercorns, and cilantro.

### 2. Make the Sauce
- In a bowl, combine sugar, white vinegar, light soy sauce, water, oyster sauce, and a little salt.
- Mix well and set aside.

### 3. Coat and Fry the Eggplant
- Drain the soaked eggplant and pat dry.
- Coat evenly with potato starch.
- Lightly sprinkle with water, then coat again. Repeat about 4 times to build a thick, even crust (key for crispiness).
- Heat oil to about 80% hot.
- Fry the eggplant, separating pieces to avoid sticking.
- Fry on high heat for about 4 minutes until golden, then remove.
- Reheat the oil and fry again for ~20 seconds for extra crispiness.

### 4. Stir-Fry and Combine
- In a clean pan, heat a little oil.
- Add garlic, green onion, dried chili, and Sichuan peppercorns. Stir-fry until fragrant.
- Pour in the prepared sauce and cook until it thickens and bubbles.
- Add the fried eggplant and toss quickly to coat evenly.
- Turn off heat, add cilantro, and mix.
- Garnish with white sesame seeds if desired.

---

## Tips
- Potato starch gives the best crispy texture.
- Repeated coating + light water spraying prevents sogginess.
- Maintain high oil temperature to keep the eggplant crispy and prevent excess oil absorption.
